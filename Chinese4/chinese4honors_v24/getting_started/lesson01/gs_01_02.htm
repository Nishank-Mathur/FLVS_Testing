Course Icons

Road signs let you know what to expect as you drive. Similarly, there will be some symbols or icons found throughout this course. Icons are used in the course to draw your attention to some specific things. Here are icons you may see.

Resources

Throughout the course, you will need support of various resources, such as a vocabulary summary or a grammar review. This icon indicates that you have some resources you will find useful for the lesson/assessment you are currently working on. You are in luck!

Reading Power

At the beginning of each content lesson, you will see the Reading Power icon. This icon lets you know that you will be reading a short paragraph in Chinese characters that you have learned previously.

Translation Globe

Throughout the course, you will see the translation globe to support you when reading Chinese characters.

Collaboration

This icon indicates that there are collaboration opportunities available for you, on which you can work together with your classmates, using the knowledge you have obtained at that point. Collaboration opportunities are often provided with choices, and you usually will have till the end of a segment to work on these collaboration projects.

Vocabulary in Action

After each vocabulary section, this icon will appear. The Vocabulary in Action section will provide sample sentences in Chinese and English—using the vocabulary you just learned.

Notes

This icon usually appears right after some new vocabulary is introduced, and the notes provide supplemental information that you should know about some of the new words. Remember that you may be assessed on the content introduced in the "notes" sections.

It’s Also Said

In English, some people say pop or soft drink, and others say soda. There are similar variations in Chinese, due to its vast use in different regions or contexts. In this course, you will also be introduced to some common terms and expressions used in different Chinese-speaking regions that bear the same meanings as the formal term in Mandarin Chinese that you are learning. You won't be required to use these on the assessments, but please feel free to keep a list of them with your vocabulary for your personal use or even to show them off in your presentational speaking and writing assignments.

Video

In this course, this icon usually appears before watching a video.

Self-Check

This icon indicates there are some practices you can do on your own. Self-check practices in this course provide clear and instant feedback for your practice. They can immediately help reinforce your learning in an effective way.

Toolbox

The toolbox is where you keep the extra tools you need to help you complete the task ahead. Your toolbox has special Chinese tools to help you complete this course. We encourage you to reach out to your teacher, but always check the toolbox first when you have questions. You will find extended explanations about the grammar or other concepts that you might need extra help with. Please take some time to explore all the tools at your fingertips.

Think and Apply

Want to show off your Chinese skills? Whenever you see the Think and Apply icon, you will have the opportunity to practice what you have learned in the lesson with family and friends. Take advantage of this opportunity of practicing your skills.

Assessment

At the end of each lesson, you will see the assessment icon and the instructions for turning in your assessment. You will be asked to complete a quiz or exam or submit an assignment for your instructor to grade.

Review the Old, Know the New

In the beginning of each content lesson, you will have the opportunity to review some old vocabulary you have learned prior to this course which you can use to form sentences with new grammar structures you are learning in that content lesson.

Course Interactives

Below are some types of interactives you will see and use in this course.

Timelines

This interactive helps you review the timeline of different products around the world.

Checkpoint Practice

This interactive helps you review the vocabulary you have learned by providing multimedia questions in various complexity levels.

If you click the hamburger (three lines with a circle) at the top-right of your screen, you can access the text version.

Grammar Sentence Forming Practice

This interactive gives you the opportunity to select words from a menu to form complete sentences—using the grammar knowledge you just learned.

Self-check Practice

This interactive allows you to show mastery of the information you just watched on the dialogue videos by practicing and giving immediate feedback.

Flip Cards

This interactive presents culture-related topics by hovering over each image with your mouse.

Grammar Tabs

This interactive presents the grammar points and example sentences, using multiple tabs.

Vocabulary Tiles

This interactive helps you study new vocabulary words by presenting them in multiple forms and providing tools for you to verbally practice them.
