Lessons & Assessments

Parts of a Lesson

Beginning

Each lesson begins with an introduction to the lesson's big idea, followed by the note guide to assist you as you work through the lesson. There will be a reading power section in which you will review the vocabulary you previously learned. There will also be a self-check to help you practice your reading comprehension.

Middle

Read each lesson carefully to understand all the concepts. Also, be sure to complete the activities. You will practice the concepts and learn from your mistakes so you can be better prepared before you complete your assessment.

End

Each lesson ends with reflections on your learning and some information about the assessment you will need to submit for a grade. You will be asked to either complete a quiz or exam or submit an assignment that will be graded by your instructor. Be sure to read any instructions that are provided.

Submitting Your Work

As you complete each lesson, you will need to submit an assessment. Assessments consist of pretests, quizzes, voice activities, assignments, Discussion-Based Assessments, and exams.

Pretest

At the beginning of each lesson, you will take a pretest of multiple-choice questions related to the content in the module to see what you already know. Don't worry; this diagnostic will have little to no impact on your grade, but it will give you a window into the knowledge you will gain throughout the module. It will also help you understand one component of how you will be assessed in the course.

Quizzes

Many lessons include a multiple-choice quiz with questions. In these types of assessments, you will choose one correct answer from the choices given. Often the question is paired with an image or data graph. To be successful, make sure to read each question and its stimuli carefully and use your skills to select the best response.

Module Exams

Modules 1, 2, and 4 will end with a multiple-choice and matching exam that assesses your understanding.

Voice Activities

Lesson 3 for all mmodules will have a voice activity that you will need to complete. For this assignment, you will need to record and submit an audio in complete sentences in Chinese.

Presentational Speaking

Lesson 8 for all modules will be a presentational speaking activity that you will need to complete. For this assignment, you will need to record and submit an audio or a video, using complete sentences in Chinese.

Discussion-Based Assessments

The discussion-based assessment (or DBA) is an opportunity to discuss your skills and learning objectives in each module with your instructor. This isn't a "one size fits all" activity. Each DBA is a specific activity and discussion. It provides opportunities for you to make connections in your own learning across key concepts, ideas, and policies. You can ask questions and demonstrate your growing skills in Chinese.

Segment Exam

The segment exam at the end of each segment in the course will assess your understanding of the course as a whole. It will include multiple-choice and matching questions.

Collaboration

Collaboration assessments are peer project opportunities. You will have the opportunity to participate in a complex, multi-step, problem-solving activity and share feedback with at least one other student to benefit from a peer's perspective. There are synchronous and asynchronous opportunities, meaning you do not have to be working with your peers at the exact same time, but you may. However, you must complete the collaboration assessment before taking the segment exam. There are opportunities provided in the collaboration section. Contact your instructor early about collaboration opportunities.

Workshops

The workshops will give you an opportunity to demonstrate your knowledge of Chinese 4 through a host of interesting and creative activities such as creating a menu in Chinese. In each workshop you will also have the opportunity to learn and practice handwriting twenty Chinese characters and use them in your work. These workshop activities will be submitted to your instructor and graded according to a rubric.

Rubrics

Certain assignments contain a rubric that your teacher will use to grade your assignment. The rubric shows you exactly the kind of work your teacher will expect from you. Before beginning your assignment, read through the rubric to understand the expectations of the assignment. Use it to guide you as you complete your assignment. Then, before you submit your work, compare it against the rubric to make sure you’re turning in the best work possible.
