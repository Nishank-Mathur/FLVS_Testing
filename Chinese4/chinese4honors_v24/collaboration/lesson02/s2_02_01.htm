STOP AND COLLABORATE

Art can be found everywhere. For this collaboration, you will have the opportunity to research and discuss two forms of art from the Chinese culture.

This collaboration has three parts:

Answer the following questions and record your answers

Select two forms of art from the Chinese culture.

  • Research and review two forms of art from the Chinese culture.
  • Write down at least four important details about the two forms of art and explain briefly four important details or pieces of information about the two forms of art you selected.
  • Write down additional information (at least four examples) about the two forms of art you selected: Where did each form of art originate? What positive impact does each form of art have? Are there other forms of art similar to the ones you selected? What is your viewpoint about each form of art?

* Remember, you will conduct brief, online research before starting this collaboration.

Include the following:

  • Give three sentences in Chinese, introducing yourself. Include (in complete sentences) your name, where you live, and how old you are.
  • Prepare one paragraph of at least four to five sentences in English, describing the important details and additional important information about the two forms of art you selected.
  • Create a seven-question survey of questions to ask your partner. The first three questions should be in Chinese, asking your partner their name, where they live, and how old they are. The remaining four questions should be in English, asking your partner about which two forms of art from the Chinese culture they chose, the important details they discovered, and any additional information about the two forms of art they selected.
Find a Collaboration Partner

Find a partner to work with. Contact your instructor for assistance with finding a partner. Once you have found someone to work with, you and your partner will communicate back and forth to complete the collaboration task.

Be sure to meet all the requirements in the applicable collaboration rubric.

Submit Your Collaboration Reflection and the Final Collaboration Product

When you have completed both parts one and two, you will need to complete the Collaboration Reflection Worksheet and turn it in along with your completed collaboration project. The collaboration reflection is the evidence of the collaboration, along with a peer evaluation and self-evaluation.
