STOP AND COLLABORATE

Every community has contributions in our world. For this collaboration, you will have the opportunity to research and discuss one contribution from the Chinese culture in your country or in the world and how that contribution improves society (jobs, language, etc.).

This collaboration has three parts:

Answer the following questions and record your answers

Select one contribution from the Chinese culture in your country or in the world.

  • Research and review one contribution from the Chinese culture in your country or in the world and how that contribution improves society (jobs, language, etc.).
  • Write down at least four important details about the contribution and explain briefly four important details or information about the contribution you selected.
  • Write down additional information (at least four examples) about the contribution you selected: How did it arrive to your country or place(s) in the world? What positive impact does the contribution have? Is this similar to another contribution in your country or place(s) in the world, or is it different from another contribution in your country or place(s) in the world?

* Remember, you will conduct brief, online research before starting this collaboration.

Include the following:

  • Give three sentences in Chinese, introducing yourself. Include (in complete sentences) your name, where you live, and how old you are.
  • Prepare one paragraph of at least four to five sentences in English, describing the important details and additional important information about the contribution you selected.
  • Create a seven-question survey of questions to ask your partner. The first three questions should be in Chinese, asking your partner their name, where they live, and how old they are. The remaining four questions should be in English, asking your partner about which Chinese contribution they chose, the important details they discovered, and any additional information about the contribution they selected.
Find a Collaboration Partner

Find a partner to work with. Contact your instructor for assistance with finding a partner. Once you have found someone to work with, you and your partner will communicate back and forth to complete the collaboration task.

Be sure to meet all the requirements in the applicable collaboration rubric.

Submit your collaboration reflection and the final collaboration product

When you have completed both parts one and two, you will need to complete the Collaboration Reflection Worksheet and turn it in along with your completed collaboration project. The collaboration reflection is the evidence of the collaboration, along with a peer evaluation and self-evaluation.
